ARC / Layer 02

See what's happening in the crop, not just the image.

ARC is a physics-grounded crop model that goes beyond vegetation indices. It uses radiative transfer inversion to extract actual biophysical parameters — leaf area, canopy structure, chlorophyll content — and tracks how they change through the growing season.

What it does

Crop parameters, not pixels.

Most remote sensing products give you a vegetation index. ARC gives you the physical quantities that actually describe crop condition.

The problem

Vegetation indices are noisy shortcuts.

NDVI and similar indices collapse multi-spectral information into a single number. They saturate at moderate canopy cover, they're sensitive to soil background, and they don't tell you which part of the crop is changing or why.

How ARC works

Radiative transfer inversion on multi-spectral data.

ARC models how light interacts with leaf tissue, canopy structure, and soil — then inverts that model against satellite observations to recover the biophysical properties that best explain the signal. This works across multiple spectral bands simultaneously, extracting more information than any single index can.

What you get

Interpretable crop intelligence through the season.

Leaf area index, canopy cover, chlorophyll content, and other crop parameters mapped over time. You can see not just that something changed, but what changed and how it relates to crop development.

In the stack

ARC is the interpretation layer.

A

Between clean signal and outcome

Layer 02 of 03

ARC works best on SIAC-corrected reflectance, but can also process other analysis-ready data. Downstream, it feeds the Yield layer with crop-state evidence that makes yield forecasts more interpretable. ARC also works standalone for teams whose primary need is crop monitoring.
