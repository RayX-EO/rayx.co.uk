Yield / Layer 03

Yield forecasts you can explain.

Yield is the outcome layer in the RayX stack. It translates crop-state intelligence into the numbers decision-makers need: expected production, deviation from normal, and early signals of shortfall or surplus — with a visible chain back to what's happening in the field.

What it does

From crop evidence to production outlook.

Most yield forecasts are either too late (waiting for ground data) or too opaque (ML predictions without explanation). Yield sits in the middle ground.

The problem

Yield predictions without context are hard to act on.

When a model says "wheat yield is down 8%," the natural follow-up is "why?" and "how confident are you?" If the model can't answer those questions with traceable evidence, the prediction becomes just another opinion in a noisy market.

How Yield works

Crop-state parameters drive the forecast.

Instead of correlating satellite indices with historical yield numbers, Yield uses the biophysical crop parameters from ARC — leaf area, canopy development, stress indicators — as inputs to production estimation. The reasoning connects back to real physical quantities.

What you get

Yield signals with an evidence trail.

Production direction, regional comparison, and risk flags. When the forecast moves, you can trace it back to which crop parameters changed, in which areas, and when — not just that a number shifted.
