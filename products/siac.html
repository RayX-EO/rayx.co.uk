SIAC / Layer 01

What it does

Atmospheric correction, done properly.

Satellite sensors measure light that has passed through the atmosphere twice. SIAC models that journey using radiative transfer physics to recover what the surface actually looks like.

The problem

Raw satellite data isn't ready for analysis.

Clouds, haze, aerosol loads, and water vapour change what the sensor sees from day to day and place to place. Without correction, comparing images from different dates or sensors is unreliable.

How SIAC works

Physics-based atmospheric modelling.

SIAC estimates the atmospheric state for each image using a combination of auxiliary data and radiative transfer inversion. It then removes the atmospheric contribution to produce surface reflectance — without relying on machine learning or empirical look-up tables.

What you get

Reflectance you can trust and compare.

Consistent surface reflectance across Sentinel-2, Landsat, and selected commercial sensors. A stable input layer for vegetation monitoring, change detection, crop modelling, and any downstream analytics.
