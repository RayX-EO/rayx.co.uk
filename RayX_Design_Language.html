RayX Design Language

A visual system rooted in the physics of light — how different wavelengths reveal what the eye alone cannot see.

Brand Guidelines · V1.0 · April 2026

1. Brand Philosophy

The name RayX encodes our core science. A ray of light appears white to the eye, but a prism splits it into a full spectrum of wavelengths. Each wavelength reveals different information about the world — and that is exactly what our technology does with satellite imagery.

The three spectrum colors — Red, Blue, and Green — map directly to the multi-spectral bands we use to observe crops and landscapes from space. Together they form the "X" in RayX: the intersection of physics, data, and insight.

Red Band

620 – 750 nm · Visible Red / Near Infrared

Chlorophyll absorption, vegetation stress detection, crop health monitoring

Blue Band

450 – 520 nm · Visible Blue

Atmospheric correction, water body mapping, soil and vegetation discrimination

Green Band

520 – 600 nm · Visible Green

Canopy reflectance, plant vigour assessment, biomass estimation

Design Principles

◓

Decompose to Reveal

Like splitting white light through a prism, RayX takes the complex and separates it into clear, understandable signals. Our design should feel clarifying — never cluttered.

◎

Ground in Physics

Our science is physically grounded, and our visual language should be too. Precision over decoration. Data-rich without being data-heavy. Every element earns its place.

⊕

Multi-band Thinking

No single view tells the whole story. Our three spectrum colours work together — in charts, layouts, and data visualisations — to represent complementary dimensions of insight.

4. Logo Usage

The RayX logo exists in two forms: the full wordmark (preferred) and the icon mark (for small spaces, favicons, app icons). The X is always rendered with the four spectrum segments — it is never a plain letter.

RayX
Primary Wordmark White on Navy — default usage for slides, documents, headers
RayX
Reversed Wordmark Navy on White — for light backgrounds, print documents
Icon Mark For app icons, favicons, social avatars, small placements
RayX
Over Photography White wordmark with subtle shadow — use over satellite/field imagery

Clear Space & Minimum Size

Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "R" on all sides. Minimum wordmark width: 80px on screen, 20mm in print. Minimum icon size: 24px / 6mm.

Logo Don'ts

Don't
Recolour the X segments — the White/Red/Blue/Green arrangement is fixed and maps to specific spectral bands.
Don't
Place the logo on a busy, high-contrast photograph without a darkening overlay or container.
Don't
Rotate, stretch, or apply effects (shadows, outlines, gradients) to the logo mark itself.
Don't
Use a single spectrum colour as a standalone brand colour — always use the full triad or navy as the primary.

5. Design Elements

Recurring visual motifs that reinforce the light-decomposition concept across all touchpoints.

The Spectrum Bar

The signature element: a thin gradient strip in Red → Blue → Green. Use as a footer accent on slides, a header underline on documents, or a section divider. It represents the decomposed light beam — the core of the RayX identity.

Full width — slide footer, document header

Segmented — data categories

Short accent — next to headings

Gradients

Navy Hero

Primary slide/page backgrounds. Deep → Navy → Light at 135°.

Full Spectrum

Feature highlights, CTA buttons, hero accents. Use sparingly.

Blue → Green

Data visualisations: water-to-land, atmosphere-to-canopy.

Red → Amber

Heat maps, stress indicators, attention-drawing overlays.

Iconography Style

Use outlined, geometric icons with 1.5–2px stroke weight. Icons should feel technical and precise, not playful or illustrative. Preferred icon sets: Lucide, Phosphor, or custom line icons.

Photography & Imagery

Prefer satellite imagery, aerial photography of farmland, and macro shots of crops. When using decorative images, apply a Navy overlay at 60–80% opacity to maintain text legibility and brand consistency. Avoid generic stock photography — images should feel scientific and grounded.
