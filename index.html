How it works

Three layers that build on each other.

Most crop intelligence tools skip straight to an answer. RayX keeps the chain visible: clean the satellite signal first, interpret what it means for the crop, then project the outcome. You can use one layer or all three.

Layer 01

SIAC

Atmospheric correction

Satellite images are distorted by the atmosphere. SIAC corrects for this using a physical model of how light travels through air, water vapour, and aerosols — producing surface reflectance you can compare across sensors and dates.

  • Corrects Sentinel-2, Landsat, and commercial imagery.
  • Removes atmospheric noise without machine learning guesswork.
  • Creates a stable foundation for everything downstream.
Learn more about SIAC →
Layer 02

ARC

Crop state intelligence

ARC is a physics-grounded crop model that translates multi-spectral reflectance into biophysical crop parameters: leaf area, canopy structure, chlorophyll, and growth stage.

  • Goes beyond vegetation indices to actual crop properties.
  • Tracks development through the growing season.
Learn more about ARC →
Layer 03

Yield

Forecasting & risk

Yield translates crop-state intelligence into the numbers that drive planning: expected production, deviation from normal, and early warning of shortfall or surplus.

  • Pre-harvest yield direction grounded in crop evidence.
  • Risk signals you can trace back to what happened in the field.
