Our story

From research to product.

The problem

Satellite data is abundant. Reliable crop answers are not.

There's no shortage of satellite imagery. The hard part is turning it into crop information that people can trust enough to make decisions on — across different sensors, regions, and growing seasons.

Our approach

Build on physics instead of shortcuts.

RayX's technology is rooted in radiative transfer theory: mathematical models of how light interacts with the atmosphere, soil, and plant canopies. This gives us outputs that are explainable and consistent, not just statistically fitted to training data.

The result

A connected chain from satellite signal to agricultural decision.

SIAC corrects the atmosphere. ARC interprets the crop. Yield projects the outcome. Each layer feeds the next, and the reasoning stays visible throughout.

What we believe

Principles that shape our work.

Transparency over complexity

If you can't explain why a model produces a given output, the output isn't trustworthy enough for real decisions. Our physics-based approach keeps the reasoning chain visible.

Accuracy over speed-to-market

We'd rather deliver a reliable signal next month than an unreliable one this week. The agricultural sector needs tools it can depend on season after season.

Foundation

Earth Observation Research

RayX was founded by researchers with deep expertise in radiative transfer modelling, atmospheric correction algorithms, and satellite-based crop monitoring. The team has contributed to ESA-funded projects and peer-reviewed research in remote sensing science.
